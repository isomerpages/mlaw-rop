<p>Dear licensee,</p>
<p></p>
<p>The Financial Action Task Force (“<strong>FATF</strong>”) has published
reports on the money laundering, terrorism financing and proliferation
financing (“<strong>ML/TF/PF</strong>”) risks posed by new and emerging
technologies and rising trends in such technologies, in particular, a <strong>Horizon Scan on Artificial Intelligence (“AI”) and Deepfakes</strong>,
and a <strong>Targeted Report on Stablecoins and Unhosted Wallets</strong>.</p>
<p></p>
<p>The FATF reports highlight how these technologies may be exploited by
illicit actors and underscore the need for regulated entities to remain
vigilant to the risks that these technologies pose.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>What are the rising trends in these technologies?</strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<p>There is a rising trend where <strong>AI enabled deepfakes</strong>&nbsp;(e.g.,
videos, images or audio created using AI techniques) are <strong>now more widely used</strong>,
including to mimic real people’s appearance, voice or actions to impersonate
individuals and/or facilitate fraud and other illicit activities.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Stablecoins</strong>, including through <strong>unhosted wallets</strong>&nbsp;(i.e.,
wallets that do not involve a virtual asset service provider (“<strong>VASP</strong>”)
or financial institution (“<strong>FI</strong>”) subject to anti-money
laundering, countering financing of terrorism and countering proliferation
financing (“<strong>AML/CFT/CPF</strong>”) obligations), <strong>have increasingly become a common component of ML, TF and PF schemes that use virtual assets (“VAs”)</strong>. <strong>Stablecoins </strong>generally
refer to a type of VA, and can be used as a means of payment and/or store
of value. Stablecoins have a mechanism (e.g., linkage to reference assets
such as fiat currencies or other VAs) with which they purport to maintain
price stability.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Why are these rising trends a concern?</strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Once rare, <strong>deepfakes</strong>&nbsp;have become increasingly prevalent
and can be used to circumvent AML/CFT/CPF controls, particularly customer
due diligence (“<strong>CDD</strong>”) systems and measures. Deepfakes
can be used to <strong>impersonate individuals and manipulate biometric authentication</strong>,
a concern given a growing reliance on biometric verification. They can
be used to commit ML/TF/PF, and such technologies are also being used in
consumer fraud schemes and phishing attacks. <strong>Generative AI</strong>&nbsp;can
also be used to create fake documents that can facilitate fraud and deception,
including by creating false documentation so that transactions or economic
activities appear real.</p>
<p></p>
<p>While <strong>stablecoins</strong>&nbsp;have the same vulnerabilities as
other VAs, stablecoins are more likely to be used in peer-to-peer <strong>(“P2P”)</strong>&nbsp;transactions
due to their price stability and ample liquidity. Conducted without the
involvement of AML/CFT/CPF-obliged intermediaries, P2P transactions <strong>via unhosted wallets</strong>&nbsp;are
exposed to heightened ML/TF/PF risk. Reports indicate that stablecoins
are the most popular VA used in illicit transactions, and the FATF has
observed that the <strong>use of stablecoins by illicit actors has continued to increase over time</strong>.
Stablecoins have become increasingly attractive to illicit actors due to
their <strong>liquidity, interoperability and ease of cross-border transfer</strong>.
Illicit actors may collect illicit proceeds in the form of stablecoins
or <strong>convert laundered funds into stablecoins</strong>, before exchanging
them into fiat currency.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>What can you do?</strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<p>Licensees should:</p>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Keep abreast of evolving cyber risks and threats, relevant advisories,
and ensure your risk mitigation measures, internal controls, staff training,
and technological and system defences are reviewed and enhanced where necessary.
You are encouraged to review how cyber-enabled threats may impact how you
fulfil your AML/CFT/CPF obligations, such as CDD/ enhanced CDD (“<strong>ECDD</strong>”)
measures.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Regularly review your risk assessment and internal policies, procedures
and controls to align with AML/CFT/CPF requirements and ensure they remain
updated and relevant, addressing risks posed by evolving technology – including
but not limited to GenAI and deepfake technologies.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p>Licensees may also refer to earlier papers published by the Monetary Authority
of Singapore (“<strong>MAS</strong>”) concerning cyber risks associated
with <a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>generative artificial intelligence (</u></a><strong><a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>“GenAI”</u></a></strong>
<a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-generative-artificial-intelligence" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>)</u>
</a>&nbsp;and <a href="https://www.mas.gov.sg/regulation/circulars/cyber-risks-associated-with-deepfakes" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>deepfakes</u></a>.</p>
<p></p>
<p>Licensees are also reminded of:</p>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>The obligation under Part 2 of the Third Schedule of the Pawnbrokers Act
2015 (“<strong>PBA</strong>”) to <strong>conduct CDD</strong>, including
before the pawnbroker makes a relevant loan, relevant transaction or where
the pawnbroker has reason to suspect ML/TF/PF, and to <strong>conduct ECDD</strong>&nbsp;when
they have reason to believe that a pawner or customer, a person on whose
behalf the pawner or customer is acting, a beneficial owner of the person,
or a relevant loan or relevant transaction may present a <strong>high risk of ML/TF/PF</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Licensees should carefully assess the risks of loans or transactions involving
payment in stablecoins or other digital payment tokens, conduct CDD and
ECDD where required, and <strong>file a suspicious transaction report (“STR”)</strong>&nbsp;in
a timely manner if circumstances exist that require the licensee to do
so.</p>
</li>
</ul>
